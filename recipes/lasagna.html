Lasagna Recipe

Description

When I was in the Caribbean on vacation when I was about 12 years old, my parents took me to this famous Italian restaurant where they served the best lasagna imaginable. I have been chasing that lasagna for almost 15 years. I live in NY too and been to Italy several times so I’ve been to all the great Italian restaurants - nothing holds a candle to that lasagna. I’ve also tried so many lasagna recipes and I’ve never been able to find the lasagna that comes close to that one I tried so many years ago - until today.

Ok so this lasagna is really a 2-day recipe at best but if you don’t have time you can probably shorten it to one day. This recipe is modified based on a few different lasagna recipes and tips and tricks I’ve learned on the way through my lasagna quest. Obviously lasagna is amazing for meal prep because it makes so much. Also you can prepare the whole lasagna and then freeze it and bake it later.

Cooking time: approx 2 days for best results, 1 hour active cooking time; 12 servings, about $2-$3 each

Ingredients

Meat Sauce

  • 1 lb 85/15 ground beef
  • 1 lb mild Italian pork sausage
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 large carrot or 2 small carrots, diced
  • 4 oz mushrooms, diced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3/4 cup red cooking wine
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh basil, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • pinch of red pepper flakes
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning

Cheese Filling

  • 1lb whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tbsp fresh basil, chopped
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/8 tsp ground nutmeg

Additional Ingredients

  • 1 package lasagna noodles (16 oz) Note: Don't use no-boil noodles for this recipe, they won't cook properly and will result in a mushy lasagna.
  • 1 lb fresh mozzarella cheese, grated
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese for garnish
  • 2 tbsp fresh basil for garnish

Instructions

Meat Sauce

Note: Pepare a day ahead and leave it in the fridge for up to 24 hours. This will enchance the flavor and allow the meat to absorb more of the sauce.

  1. Heat a large dutch oven over medium heat and add the olive oil.
  2. Add onion, carrot, and mushroom and coook for 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute or until fragrant.
  4. Add ground beef and sausage and cook until browned
  5. Add red wine and bring the heat up to high for 2-3 minutes
  6. Add remaining ingredients
  7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for an hour and a half, stirring occassionally.
  8. Allow to cool, then refigerate for 24 hours
